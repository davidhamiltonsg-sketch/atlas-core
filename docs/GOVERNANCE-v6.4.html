BTC is being transitioned into IBIT like-for-like: BTC and IBIT are governed as a single Bitcoin sleeve — combined target 7%, combined cycle-aware hard cap 8% (§4.1) — never 7% each. Core principle — a loss is never a sell signal. BTC is a held conviction asset. When underweight it is accumulated on weakness toward target under its cap — never liquidated. The SGOV buffer is established from new monthly contributions, never by selling.
SGOV — FX risk acknowledgement. SGOV is denominated in USD and holds US Treasury bills. Because portfolio metrics are tracked in SGD, a declining USD/SGD rate can temporarily erode the buffer's real value. This is accepted by design: SGOV's job is market-shock absorption and dry powder, not SGD yield optimisation. Do not substitute a SGD-denominated cash instrument — the shock-buffer function requires USD liquidity aligned with the portfolio's base currency.

§1.2 — SGOV Drawdown Rules NEW

The buffer is spent only under the three conditions below. Outside them, SGOV is held and replenished from contributions. No ambiguity is left about when the buffer gets drawn down.

ConditionWhen it appliesHow it is drawn
1 · Policy-shock deploymentAn A1 trigger fires (>~10% drop from a discrete event) and the 14-day wait has elapsed.Deploy the buffer in tranches (30 / 40 / 30) into the most beaten-down quality holding. Nothing else is sold.
2 · Hard-cap trim parkingProceeds arise from a mandated §2 / §4 hard-cap trim.Park the proceeds in SGOV temporarily, then redeploy per the §5 contribution engine. A holding bay — not new spending.

Macro shock (A2) explicitly holds SGOV. A sustained decline is not a drawdown trigger — keep the buffer intact and keep contributing. Any reason not listed above is disallowed.

5The Contribution Engine

Cadence. Monthly contribution of $3,000, plus an annual January lump sum of $20,000, 5% annual contribution growth. Currency USD; brokerage IBKR Singapore; contribution date the 15th.

§5.4 — Monthly Decision Engine (under five minutes, in order)

  1. Hard cap breached? → execute the mandated trim immediately (BTC > cycle cap → trim; QQQM >30% → halt + trim; SMH >12% → trim to 10%; combined QQQM+SMH >42% → trim; Nvidia >13% → reduce cluster). Then step 6.
  2. Any asset below target? → direct 100% of the contribution to the most underweight asset. Then step 6.
  3. Any asset above its soft band? → pause contributions there, redirect to underweights (unless §4 overrides). Then step 6.
  4. Structural-review trigger active? (§4.4) → schedule a review within 30 days; keep contributing. Then step 6.
  5. Normal deployment → split at target weights: VT 52 · QQQM 23 · SMH 10 · VWO 8 · BTC 7. Then step 6.
  6. Drawdown >25% from ATH? → activate the Crash Protocol (§6.2). Continue DCA unchanged. No discretionary sales.
  7. Compliance confirmation → within dealing window, contribution executed, governance log updated, drift + concentration reviewed.
  8. System closure → close. Do not monitor daily. Reopen only at the next scheduled review.

Market-aware DCA overlay

On top of §5.4 the router: skips any position within ~3% of its 52-week high (VT exempt — the continuous anchor); deploys into confirmed dips (≥12% off high) via the three-tranche rule 30% / 40% / 30%; treats an underweight conviction holding (incl. BTC) as eligible; never feeds an overweight position; and shows an on-screen note when the plan adapts.

With SMH at its 52-week high (~$669), QQQM extended, VWO near its high, and BTC underweight, the engine in June 2026 reasons as follows:

The old drift-only engine would have added to all three; the market-aware engine redirects the capital to better entries.

8Compliance

  • Manual execution only — inside approved dealing windows, with employer pre-approval where firm policy requires it.
  • Monthly execution cadence — confirm dealing window → review allocation vs target → check look-through concentration → generate drift-adjusted plan → execute and log each transaction (date, asset, amount, price) → update the intelligence log.
  • Dealing window (defined) — opens the 3rd business day after the 15th and closes the last business day of that month. All routine execution happens inside this window.
    Hard-cap breach exception: a mandated trim executes on the first available business day after the breach is confirmed (outside the window if necessary), with a mandatory governance-log entry.
  • Emergency-reserve rule — adequate reserves held outside the portfolio at all times. The portfolio is never the emergency fund. No withdrawals before 2045 except documented extraordinary circumstances.

10Precedence & the Next Best Move

The order of laws (absolute): §4 Look-Through Concentration → §3 Drift Governance → §5 Contribution Engine → all other sections.
Concentration always overrides conviction · hard triggers always override soft alerts · a paper loss never overrides anything.

Next Best Move — the engine's decision ladder

The single highest-priority action is the first rung that fires:

  1. Hard cap / concentration breach → TRIM to target. Enforces the combined QQQM+SMH ceiling (>42%), the SMH cap (12%), and the floating BTC cap (8% in the Normal phase).
  2. Defensive gap (buffer <8%) → BUILD SGOV from new contributions — never by selling.
  3. Market opportunity (confirmed dip) → DEPLOY tranche 1 into the dip.
  4. Conviction underweight (e.g. BTC below target) → ACCUMULATE on weakness toward target — never sell at a loss.
  5. Hard drift underweight → FILL the biggest gap.
  6. Soft drift → REDIRECT the contribution.
  7. Healthy → STANDARD DCA (skip anything at a 52-week high).

There is never a "nothing to do" that leaves the user guessing — if everything is healthy, the move is "keep doing your standard DCA."

13Behavioural Pre-Commitments

Binding rules decided in advance, so no decision is made in the heat of a shock. Where two conflict, the most conservative (do-less, sell-nothing) interpretation wins. The larger long-term threat is not the shock — it is well-intentioned tinkering.

A Shock response (decided before the shock)
A1Policy shock.
On a >~10% drop from a discrete event (tariff, geopolitics, regulation): take no action for 14 calendar days, then deploy the buffer into the most beaten-down quality holding in tranches. Sell nothing.
A2Macro shock.
On a sustained decline (rate cycle, recession, de-rating): hold everything, continue scheduled contributions unchanged, do not redesign. The 2022 rule.
A3A loss is never a sell trigger.
Being down is a sunk cost. Sell a conviction holding only when its thesis breaks — never the colour of the number. Ask: "Would I buy this at today's price?" If yes, do not sell.

Broken-thesis criteria (per conviction asset):

QQQM: breaks if US mega-cap tech structurally loses pricing power for 3+ consecutive years and a credible non-US digital-economy alternative emerges at equivalent scale. Valuation de-rating, a rate cycle, or a single bad earnings year do not break it.

SMH: breaks if semiconductor capex growth reverses for 3+ consecutive quarters and AI-compute demand enters a sustained structural decline — not a cyclical inventory correction. Cyclical downturns within a secular upcycle do not break it.

BTC / IBIT (Bitcoin sleeve): breaks if (a) a critical protocol-layer vulnerability is confirmed unresolved, or (b) sovereign adoption reverses across 3+ major economies simultaneously with no recovery path, or (c) a technically superior monetary-layer replacement achieves a dominant network effect. Single-jurisdiction regulatory pressure, price drawdown alone, or short-cycle sentiment do not break it.
A4Never crystallise a loss to fund something else.
Buffers, new positions and rebalances are funded from new contributions — never by selling an underwater position.
B Buying discipline
B1Don't buy the top.
Do not add to any position within ~3% of its 52-week high. VT (the broad-market anchor) is exempt. Redirect skipped money to the anchor or hold it.
B2Three-tranche entry.
Never deploy full intended capital on the first signal. Split 30% / 40% / 30%: 30% on the first dip, 40% after three green weeks from the trough, 30% once the uptrend confirms.
B3Accumulate conviction on weakness.
An underweight conviction holding (e.g. BTC below its 7% target) is accumulated on weakness toward target, under its cap — never exited for being down.
C Structural limits
C1Hard caps are inviolable.
SMH ≤ 12%, BTC ≤ its cycle cap, combined QQQM+SMH ≤ 42% (§4.3). A breach triggers a trim back to target. No debate, no exceptions.
C2Maintain an 8–10% defensive buffer at all times once built.
Built only from new contributions (see A4).
C3No half-convictions.
Every position is either sized to matter when it wins or exited. No orphan positions too small to help and large enough to drag.
D Anti-tinkering throttles (protection from self)
D1One discretionary change per quarter, maximum.
Rule-mandated actions (cap trims, scheduled DCA) are unlimited. Discretionary changes — new ideas, re-weights, additions — are capped at one per calendar quarter.
D272-hour cooling-off.
Any action not already mandated by a written rule waits 72 hours before execution. Most urges do not survive three days.
D3New positions require a written thesis and a one-quarter wait.
No impulse tickers. The five/six-position structure is the default and changing it is deliberately slow.
E Cadence
E1The monthly 5-minute check.
Once a month: check caps (trim if breached) → check underweights (route the contribution) → check the scheduled-events calendar → act only if a rule fires. Otherwise, close the app.
Pre-decide behaviour, automate away the chances to override it, and keep a buffer that needs no prediction. Shocks recover; accumulated tinkering does not.

BAppendix — Horizon Transition Framework NEW · v6.2

Atlas Core is an accumulation system. At T-minus 5 years (2040) a mandatory transition review is triggered — not before. The review converts the framework from a contribution-maximising, volatility-tolerant accumulation structure into a sequenced drawdown plan. The accumulation rules (DCA, conviction holds, crash protocol) remain in force until that review is complete and a Distribution Governance Document is ratified.
