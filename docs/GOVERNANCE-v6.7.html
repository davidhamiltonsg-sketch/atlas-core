Governance Document

This document is the rulebook for a single retirement portfolio with a target date of 2045. Every decision — what to buy, when to buy it, when to hold, and when to trim — is made by a fixed set of rules written here. Your job is not to make those decisions. Your job is to execute the instruction this system produces, once a month, inside the monthly trading window. The rules exist so that fear, excitement, and market noise never get a vote.

Six positions. Five are long-term holdings, each with a specific job. The sixth — SGOV — is a cash-like buffer held in short-term US Treasury bills. It earns a small yield (~3.85%) and sits ready to deploy when markets drop sharply. It is not an investment; it is dry powder.

Bitcoin is a hold, not a trade. IBIT (the Bitcoin ETF) is a conviction position. If it drops below its target weight, you buy more — you don't sell it. The SGOV buffer is built from your monthly contributions, never by selling anything in the portfolio.
SGOV currency note. SGOV holds US dollars. Your portfolio is tracked in SGD. If the USD weakens against the SGD, the buffer is worth slightly less in local terms. This is accepted — SGOV's job is to be available when markets crash, not to optimise for SGD returns. Don't replace it with a SGD cash product.
When can you spend the SGOV buffer? Four situations only: (1) Sharp market drop: if markets fall more than 10% suddenly (a tariff shock, a geopolitical event), wait 14 days, then deploy the buffer into whichever quality holding fell most — in three tranches (30%, then 40%, then 30%). (2) Slow grind down: if the portfolio falls more than 20% over more than 30 days (ruling out a V-shaped recovery), and the buffer has not been deployed in the last 90 days, deploy tranche 1 (30%) only — hold the rest. This catches a sustained bear market that never produces a single discrete shock trigger. (3) After a forced trim: if a position exceeds its cap and you must sell some, the proceeds park in SGOV briefly then get reinvested at the next monthly window. (4) Delayed contribution: if your monthly contribution is late one month, SGOV can cover that month's DCA once only — not as a habit. In a full macro bear market (rate cycle, recession — sustained decline with no recovery signal), hold the remaining SGOV — don't deploy it all. The buffer is for shocks and confirmed opportunities, not for averaging into a market that may keep falling for years.
Why there are no bonds in this portfolio. Bonds are absent by deliberate choice, not oversight. The standard case for bonds rests on their negative correlation with equities during downturns — but that correlation broke decisively in 2022, when both stocks and bonds fell together as rates rose rapidly. That rate environment has not fully reversed. At a 19-year horizon (2026–2045), the interest rate sensitivity and duration risk of intermediate and long-term bonds is a drag without sufficient compensation at this stage of the accumulation cycle. SGOV does what bonds are meant to do — provide capital protection and dry powder — without the duration risk. Bonds enter the portfolio during the 2040–2045 glide path (see Appendix B) when the horizon shortens and the priority shifts from accumulation to capital preservation. Adding bonds now would be solving a 2035 problem in 2026.

2Maximum Sizes (Caps)

Each position has a hard ceiling. If a position grows past its cap — because the market pushed it there — you must sell enough to bring it back. This is not optional and is not a prediction the position will fall. It is protection against the damage a single oversized position can cause when it eventually corrects.

What "conviction hold" means. QQQM, SMH, and IBIT (BTC) are held with conviction. That means two things: (1) they can grow above their target weight without you selling them — you only sell when they hit the cap above; (2) you cannot sell them just because they are losing money. You only exit a conviction position if its long-term reason for existing has broken down (see §13 for the specific tests per position).

4.1Bitcoin's Flexible Cap

Bitcoin's maximum size adjusts with its market cycle, because Bitcoin behaves very differently depending on where it sits in a halving cycle. The last halving was 19 April 2024. The phase is determined by how many months have passed since that date, and how far the price has fallen from its cycle peak.

Post-halving bull phase

12–24 months after the halving

10%

Target is 8%, soft warning at 9%. The cap is wider because Bitcoin has historically returned most in the 12–24 months after a halving.

Normal phase

No special halving catalyst

8%

Target 7%, soft warning at 8%. Standard governance. Applies when more than 24 months have passed since the halving and the price is within 50% of its cycle high.

Bear / falling market

Price more than 50% below its cycle peak

6%

Target drops to 5%. The cap tightens to protect the portfolio if Bitcoin is in a deep drawdown.

Why 7%? The sizing rationale. The 7% target is calibrated deliberately — not arbitrary, and not borrowed from a template. It is large enough to be meaningful: a 3× return on 7% moves the overall portfolio by 21 percentage points. It is small enough to survive a complete loss: if Bitcoin went to zero today, the portfolio loses 7% and continues. Below 5%, Bitcoin's upside becomes irrelevant to retirement outcomes — it's decorative. Above 10% (outside the post-halving bull window), Bitcoin would become the portfolio's third-largest position by weight, ahead of VWO and nearly equal to SMH, which transforms it from an optionality overlay into a structural conviction bet. That's a different portfolio with a different risk profile. 7% — flexing to 8–10% in the bull phase — is the range that captures asymmetric upside without making the portfolio dependent on Bitcoin performing.

5How Each Monthly Contribution Gets Invested

Monthly schedule. $3,000 per month, plus a $20,000 lump sum every January, growing at 5% per year. Currency is USD. Broker is IBKR Singapore. Contribution date is the 15th of each month.

The monthly decision — run through this in order, stop at the first step that applies

  1. Is any position over its hard cap? → Trim it back immediately. (BTC over its cycle cap → trim; QQQM over 30% → trim; SMH over 12% → trim back to 10%; QQQM + SMH combined over 42% → trim SMH first; Nvidia over 13% → reduce.) Then go to step 8.
  2. Is any position below its target? → Put 100% of this month's contribution into the most underweight position. Then go to step 8.
  3. Is any position above its comfortable range? → Stop adding to that position. Redirect the contribution to whichever position is most underweight. Then go to step 8.
  4. Has a concentration review been triggered? → Schedule a review within 30 days. Keep contributing normally in the meantime. Then go to step 8.
  5. Everything looks normal? → Split the contribution across the five positions at their target weights: VT 52% · QQQM 23% · SMH 10% · VWO 8% · BTC 7%. Then go to step 8.
  6. Is the portfolio down more than 25% from its all-time high? → Follow the Crash Protocol (§6). Keep your monthly contributions going. Don't sell anything.
  7. Confirm the trade → Execute within the dealing window, update the governance log, check drift and concentration.
  8. Close the app. Don't monitor prices daily. Come back next month.

One additional rule: don't buy at the top

Before investing in any position, the system checks whether it is trading within 3% of its 52-week high. If it is, it skips that position and puts the money elsewhere. (VT is the one exception — you accumulate it continuously regardless of price, because it's your stable anchor.) If a position has dropped 12% or more from its recent high, that's a confirmed buying opportunity — money goes in using the three-tranche method (30% now, 40% after three green weeks, 30% once the recovery confirms).

Example — June 2026

With SMH at its 52-week high (~$669), QQQM extended, VWO near its high, and BTC at target weight, the engine in June 2026 would reason as follows:

SMH, QQQM, and VWO receive nothing this month — not because they are bad holdings, but because buying at the top is the worst entry. The market-aware engine skips overbought positions and redirects capital to better entries.

Markets create fear and excitement. Both are enemies of long-term performance. These rules exist to remove emotion from decisions before it gets the chance to cost you money.

6BSwitching ETF Vehicles

Sometimes a better version of the same investment becomes available — lower fees, better tax treatment, or a more suitable structure. This section covers how to switch from one ETF to another without it counting as a strategy change. The key principle: this system tracks what you own (Bitcoin, global equities, semiconductors), not which specific ETF holds it. Switching from one Bitcoin ETF to a better one doesn't change your Bitcoin exposure — it just improves the wrapper.

Core rule. All your position sizes, limits, buy/sell rules, and thesis tests follow the economic exposure, not the ticker. When you complete a switch, the new ETF inherits all the old ETF's rules automatically. Nothing else needs updating except the instrument name in §1.

When is a switch allowed?

A switch is a permitted governance action — it doesn't count as a discretionary strategy change — only if all three of the following are true:

#RequirementHow to verify it
1It's the same underlying thingThe new ETF tracks the same asset, index, or commodity. Check the prospectus or index methodology — not the marketing material.
2The new one is genuinely betterLower management fee, better tax structure (e.g. Irish-domiciled instead of US-domiciled), better liquidity, or lower regulatory risk. Document the reason at the time of the decision.
3Your total exposure doesn't changeThe money from selling the old ETF goes directly into the new one — not into cash, not into something else.

How to execute a switch

RuleDetail
90-day hold applies to the position, not the instrumentThe minimum hold clock runs from the original purchase date of the economic exposure — not from the date the new vehicle is bought. If the BTC position was purchased 6 months ago, it has already served its hold period regardless of which vehicle holds it post-transition.
Execute at the next dealing windowTransitions are not emergency actions. Execute within the normal monthly dealing window unless an extraordinary cost or regulatory event requires faster action (documented).
Sell-then-buy, same settlement cycle where possibleSell the old vehicle first; buy the new vehicle on the same day or the next business day. Minimise the window of being out of market. Do not wait for the old trade to settle before placing the new buy if the broker permits same-day.
No partial transitions without a completion planIf you begin a transition (sell old), you are committed to completing it (buy new) within the same dealing window. Do not create a state where both vehicles are held simultaneously beyond a single settlement cycle unless the position is being deliberately split (requires its own documented rationale).
Log the transitionRecord: date, old vehicle, new vehicle, units sold, units bought, structural reason for switch, and confirmation that the three qualifying criteria were met.
Governance continuityAll existing drift bands, caps, thesis criteria, and behavioural rules apply to the new vehicle from day one. The rule register does not require amendment for a vehicle transition — only the instrument name in §1 is updated.

8When and How to Trade

  • You execute manually. The system tells you what to do; you place the trade yourself. Where your employer's policies require pre-approval of trades, get that approval first.
  • The monthly trading window. You can only trade between the 3rd business day after the 15th of each month (giving time for your contribution to land) and the last business day of that month. Outside this window, no trades — except if a hard cap has been breached (§2, §4), in which case you act on the first available business day after the breach, and note in the log what rule triggered it.
  • Each month's checklist — open the window → compare current allocation to targets → check overall concentration → work out the drift-adjusted plan → execute each trade and log it (date, asset, amount, price) → update the log.
  • Keep an emergency fund outside this portfolio. This portfolio is never touched for emergencies. No withdrawals before 2045 except in genuinely extraordinary circumstances that you document.

10What to Do Next — the Decision Ladder

When two rules point in different directions, this is the order of priority: Hidden concentration limits (§4) beat everything → drift rules (§3) beat the contribution engine (§5) → everything else follows.
Concentration always beats conviction. Hard limits always beat soft warnings. A position losing money never overrides any rule.

The decision ladder — work down this list and stop at the first step that applies

This is what the system produces as your "next action." There's always an answer — it's never blank.

  1. A position has broken through its hard cap → TRIM IT. This covers: QQQM+SMH combined over 42%; SMH over 12%; BTC over its cycle cap. Trim back to target before doing anything else.
  2. The SGOV buffer has fallen below 8% → BUILD IT BACK UP from new contributions — never by selling anything.
  3. A quality position has dropped more than 12% from its recent high → DEPLOY tranche 1 (30%) into the dip.
  4. A conviction position (QQQM, SMH, or BTC) is below its target weight → BUY MORE on the way down toward target. Never sell it at a loss.
  5. A position has drifted significantly below target → FILL the gap with this month's contribution.
  6. A position has drifted slightly off target → REDIRECT this month's contribution toward it.
  7. Everything is on track → STANDARD MONTHLY INVESTMENT. Split the contribution at target weights — but skip any position currently within 3% of its 52-week high.

13Pre-Decided Responses to Shocks

These rules are decided now, while everything is calm, so you don't have to make decisions while panicking. When two rules conflict, the more conservative one wins — the one that involves doing less and selling nothing. The biggest long-term threat is not the shock itself. It's the well-intentioned tinkering that happens in response to it.

A Shock response (decided before the shock)
A1Policy shock.
On a >~10% drop from a discrete event (tariff, geopolitics, regulation): take no action for 14 calendar days, then deploy the buffer into the most beaten-down quality holding in tranches. Sell nothing.
A2Macro shock.
On a sustained decline (rate cycle, recession, de-rating): hold everything, continue scheduled contributions unchanged, do not redesign. The 2022 rule.
A3A loss is never a sell trigger.
Being down is a sunk cost. Sell a conviction holding only when its thesis breaks — never the colour of the number. Ask: "Would I buy this at today's price?" If yes, do not sell.

Broken-thesis criteria (per conviction asset):

QQQM: Thesis breaks if US mega-cap tech structurally loses pricing power for 3+ consecutive years AND a credible non-US digital economy alternative emerges at equivalent scale. Valuation de-rating, a rate cycle, or a single bad earnings year do not break the thesis.

SMH: Thesis breaks if semiconductor capex growth reverses for 3+ consecutive quarters AND AI compute demand enters a sustained structural decline — not a cyclical inventory correction. Cyclical downturns within a secular upcycle do not break the thesis.

BTC: Thesis breaks if (a) a critical protocol-layer vulnerability is confirmed unresolved, or (b) sovereign adoption reverses across 3+ major economies simultaneously with no recovery path, or (c) a technically superior monetary-layer replacement achieves dominant network effect. Regulatory pressure in a single jurisdiction, price drawdown alone, or short-cycle sentiment shifts do not break the thesis.
A4Never crystallise a loss to fund something else.
Buffers, new positions and rebalances are funded from new contributions — never by selling an underwater position.
B Buying discipline
B1Don't buy the top.
Do not add to any position within ~3% of its 52-week high. VT (the broad-market anchor) is exempt. Redirect skipped money to the anchor or hold it.
B2Three-tranche entry.
Never deploy full intended capital on the first signal. Split 30% / 40% / 30%: 30% on the first dip, 40% after three green weeks from the trough, 30% once the uptrend confirms.
B3Accumulate conviction on weakness.
An underweight conviction holding (e.g. BTC below its 7% target) is accumulated on weakness toward target, under its cap — never exited for being down.
C Structural limits
C1Hard caps are inviolable.
SMH ≤ 12%, BTC ≤ its cycle cap, combined QQQM+SMH ≤ 42% (§4.3). A breach triggers a trim back to target. No debate, no exceptions.
C2Maintain an 8–10% defensive buffer at all times once built.
Built only from new contributions (see A4).
C3No half-convictions.
Every position is either sized to matter when it wins or exited. No orphan positions too small to help and large enough to drag.
D Anti-tinkering throttles (protection from self)
D1One discretionary change per quarter, maximum.
Rule-mandated actions (cap trims, scheduled DCA) are unlimited. Discretionary changes — new ideas, re-weights, additions — are capped at one per calendar quarter.
D272-hour cooling-off.
Any action not already mandated by a written rule waits 72 hours before execution. Most urges do not survive three days.
D3New positions require a written thesis and a one-quarter wait.
No impulse tickers. The five/six-position structure is the default and changing it is deliberately slow.
E Cadence
E1The monthly 5-minute check.
Once a month: check caps (trim if breached) → check underweights (route the contribution) → check the scheduled-events calendar → act only if a rule fires. Otherwise, close the app.
Pre-decide behaviour, automate away the chances to override it, and keep a buffer that needs no prediction. Shocks recover; accumulated tinkering does not.

BAppendix — Plan for 2040–2045

Until 2040, everything in this document is about growing the portfolio. At five years before retirement (January 2040), a mandatory review kicks in. That review — and only that review — starts the transition from "accumulate everything" to "prepare to live off this." None of the current rules change before that date. You keep contributing, keep following the DCA rules, and keep holding conviction positions until 2040 says otherwise.

The 2040 review — what it must cover

In January 2040, block time for a structured review covering: (1) work out your retirement income target in SGD in today's money; (2) compare the portfolio's actual value to where it needs to be by 2045; (3) decide what the target allocation looks like once you're drawing it down rather than building it up; (4) write a sell-down sequence and timeline; (5) decide whether to use an annuity, a drawdown structure, or a combination, based on your tax situation and where you're living at that time. The output of this review is a new document — a Distribution Governance Document — that replaces this appendix and governs the portfolio from 2040 onwards.

When bonds enter — and why not before. Fixed income does not appear in the accumulation portfolio (2026–2041) because at a 19-year horizon, the interest rate sensitivity and duration risk of intermediate bonds costs more than it protects. The 2022 bear market — the most relevant precedent — saw both stocks and bonds fall simultaneously as rates rose, precisely when bonds were supposed to provide protection. SGOV performs the capital protection and dry-powder function without duration risk. From 2042 onward, the calculus changes: the horizon shortens, sequence-of-returns risk (the risk that a bad sequence of returns in the years just before and after retirement permanently impairs the portfolio) becomes the dominant threat, and bonds' dampening effect on annual volatility becomes more valuable than their drag on long-run returns. The 2040 review sets the actual bond vehicle, target weight, and duration profile based on the rate environment and tax situation at that time.
