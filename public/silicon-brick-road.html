Book I

Constitution

Why the system exists, what it believes, and how it governs itself.

Article IPurpose

North Star. The purpose of this Constitution is not to maximise returns. Its purpose is to maximise the probability of successfully acquiring residential property through disciplined, evidence-based investment governance while eliminating avoidable behavioural mistakes. Markets are uncertain. Governance is permanent.

The Portfolio exists to accumulate sufficient capital to support the acquisition of residential property while maintaining an acceptable level of investment risk. All investment activity shall serve this objective. Timing is flexible; the working target horizon is approximately three years, but the objective is governed by capital readiness, property opportunity, and risk conditions rather than a fixed end date.

Article IIConstitutional Principles

These seven principles are the foundation on which every operational rule rests. When two rules appear to conflict, the principle that is more conservative governs.

I
Capital exists to serve objectives. Objectives do not exist to serve capital.
II
Investment governance is more valuable than investment prediction.
III
Diversification is the primary defence against uncertainty.
IV
Risk cannot be eliminated. It can only be accepted, priced, capped, or avoided.
V
Constitutional compliance is more important than short-term performance.
VI
Markets fluctuate. The Constitution shall not.
VII
Complexity is permitted only where it demonstrably improves governance.

Article IIIGovernance Structure

Although one individual fulfils every function, governance responsibilities are separated conceptually to improve decision quality. When you review the portfolio, you are the Investment Committee. When you execute a trade, you are the Portfolio Manager. When you question the rules themselves, you are the Constitutional Reviewer. Keeping these roles distinct prevents the Portfolio Manager from quietly overruling the Committee.

Article IVInvestment Philosophy

The Portfolio has a clear view of what it does and does not believe in. These positions are permanent and not subject to revision based on market conditions.

Permanently Rejected

Market timing · Speculation · Leverage · Derivatives · Margin borrowing · Short selling · Stock picking · Narrative investing · Unapproved products · Performance chasing · Complexity without governance benefit

Permanently Accepted

Systematic investing · Passive ownership · Low turnover · Disciplined contributions · Target-driven de-risking · Behavioural controls · Evidence-based amendments · Patience as a strategy

Where returns come from. Expected returns arise from ownership of productive assets: global economic growth, corporate earnings, innovation, dividend reinvestment, and productivity improvement. They do not arise from superior forecasting. No individual or system can reliably predict short-term market movements. The Constitution governs what the Portfolio Manager does instead of predicting.

Article VAmendment Process

Amendments are rare. Market volatility alone shall never justify constitutional change. The Constitution is designed to be stable precisely when the temptation to change it is highest.

Permitted triggerExamples
Objective changesDifferent property target, changed housing strategy, target materially revised up or down.
Personal circumstancesEmployment, income, residence, contribution capacity, or family circumstances materially change.
Law or tax changesMaterial change to ETF tax treatment, US estate tax threshold, Singapore property financing rules, or MAS regulations.
Portfolio scalePortfolio exceeds SGD 500,000 or becomes part of a broader wealth architecture requiring different governance.
Superior evidenceClear, durable, evidence-based improvement to implementation that is not motivated by recent performance.
Amendment protocol. Every amendment must record: current wording · proposed wording · rationale · evidence · risks created · expected benefits · approval date · version number. A seven-day reflection period applies before any amendment takes effect, unless law or operational necessity requires immediate action. No amendment may be made during a period of market stress — this is when the Constitution is most needed and most likely to be wrongly challenged.

Operations Manual

How decisions are made, what is held, how money flows, and how trades are placed.

DefinitionsKey Terms

These terms are used precisely throughout the Constitution. When in doubt, return here.

Portfolio value
The total value of VWRA + QQQM + SMH + A35 + uninvested cash in IBKR awaiting the next A35 board lot purchase. CPF-OA is tracked separately and never included in this figure.
Portfolio peak
The highest month-end closing value the portfolio has ever reached, measured at the last business day of each month in SGD at IBKR prices. Not measured daily — only at month-end, to prevent reaction to intra-month noise.
Drawdown from peak
How far the current portfolio value is below its peak, as a percentage. Example: peak SGD 80,000, current SGD 68,000 = 15% drawdown. Checked at each monthly trading window using month-end prices.
52-week high
The highest closing price that fund has traded at on its home exchange (USD close on IBKR) over the last 12 months. Check via IBKR's price chart or the "52W High" field. Use most recent available closing price — not an intra-day price.
"Within 3% of 52-week high"
Current price ÷ 52-week high ≥ 0.97. Example: 52-week high USD 200, current USD 196 → 196/200 = 0.98 → within 3% → skip this month.
Target weights
VWRA 50% · QQQM 25% · SMH 15% · A35 10%. The sizes you aim for when everything is on track. Actual portfolio will drift — that is normal and expected.
Contribution redirect
Directing the monthly SGD 2,000 contribution to a different fund than the standard target split. Does not involve selling anything — only new money is redirected.
De-risking (Phases III–IV)
The gradual shift from stocks into A35 as the portfolio approaches SGD 120,000. Phase III involves quarterly selling of QQQM and VWRA — one of the few permitted selling actions outside the SMH cap rule.
Constitutional authority
A specific Article in this document that authorises a trade or action. Every trade logged must cite its constitutional authority. If no Article authorises an action, the action is prohibited.

Article VIDecision Engine

Every monthly contribution follows this sequence exactly. Stop immediately once a rule fires. Do not run further steps. There is always one clear answer.

Priority order. Hard limits beat everything → safety floor beats contributions → phase instructions beat market rules → drawdown rule beats underweight rule → skip-at-high rule beats underweight rule → underweight beats standard split → standard split is the default.
  1. Is SMH above 20% of the portfolio? → Sell SMH back to 15%. The only mandatory sell in the portfolio. Execute within the current trading window. Constitutional authority: Article IX.
  2. Is QQQM + SMH combined above 45%? → Halt both; direct all contributions to VWRA until the combined figure drops below 42%. Constitutional authority: Article VII.
  3. Is A35 below 7% of the portfolio? → Direct all contributions to A35 until restored above 8%. The safety floor is the priority. Constitutional authority: Article VII.
  4. Is the portfolio in Phase III or IV of de-risking? → Apply phase instructions from Article XII. Phase III involves actual selling. Phase IV halts all equity contributions. Constitutional authority: Article XII.
  5. Has the portfolio fallen more than 15% from its month-end peak? → Direct the full monthly contribution to VWRA only. Do not sell anything else. Do not change the structure. This step comes before the underweight check — in a falling market, always accumulate the diversified core. Constitutional authority: Article XI.
  6. Is QQQM or SMH within 3% of its 52-week high? → Skip that fund this month; redirect to VWRA. This is a behavioural guard against regret-buying at the peak — it is not a return-optimising claim. This step also comes before the underweight check. Constitutional authority: Article XI.
  7. Is any fund below its comfortable range? → Direct the full monthly contribution to the fund furthest below its comfortable range. Comfortable ranges are defined in Article VII: VWRA below 44%, QQQM below 20%, SMH below 11%, A35 below 7% (though A35 below 7% is governed by step 3 above). Constitutional authority: Article VII.
  8. Otherwise. → Standard DCA at target weights: VWRA 50% · QQQM 25% · SMH 15% · A35 10%. Constitutional authority: Article VII.

Why these specific funds?

Why VWRA and not the similar Vanguard fund VT? Both hold thousands of companies worldwide and perform nearly identically in terms of market exposure. The difference is domicile. VT is registered in the US; VWRA is registered in Ireland. Ireland's tax treaties mean the fund pays 15% withholding tax on US dividends (vs 30% for a US-domiciled fund) and has preferential arrangements with many other countries for non-US dividends. Combined, these treaty advantages save roughly 0.15–0.25% per year in tax drag. VWRA's annual fee is higher (0.22% vs VT's 0.07%) — the 0.15% fee gap is largely offset by the tax saving. The total net benefit of VWRA over VT for a Singapore-based investor is approximately 0.05–0.15% per year. Not dramatic — but the right choice, with clear reasoning.
Why QQQM and not the UCITS equivalent EQQQ? Both track the same 100 technology companies. QQQM charges 0.15% per year; EQQQ charges 0.33%. Since large-cap technology companies pay almost no dividends, the withholding tax saving from an Ireland-based structure barely applies. The lower-fee fund wins outright: QQQM saves 0.18% per year compared to EQQQ.
QQQM and SMH are US-registered funds — this creates a US estate tax risk that you must be aware of. When a non-US person (including a Singapore citizen without US citizenship or a US green card) holds US-registered investments, the US government can tax those assets on death. This is called US estate tax, and it applies above approximately USD 60,000 for non-US persons. Singapore does not have an estate tax treaty with the US that would reduce this threshold. At the SGD 120,000 target, your QQQM + SMH exposure (40% of portfolio) is approximately USD 35,000–40,000 — currently just under the threshold, though exchange rates affect this. As the portfolio grows, so does the exposure. VWRA is Ireland-registered and not subject to US estate tax. This risk is not a reason to avoid QQQM and SMH at current portfolio sizes, but it is a reason to: (a) monitor it annually, (b) consider switching to Ireland-registered equivalents if the portfolio grows significantly beyond SGD 200,000, and (c) seek qualified tax advice before the portfolio approaches that scale. This information is not tax or legal advice — rules change and your circumstances are specific to you.

The CHF seed — conversion protocol

The seed capital of approximately CHF 10,000 arrives from Switzerland. At current exchange rates (CHF trades at roughly 10–12% premium to USD; USD/SGD approximately 1.35), CHF 10,000 converts to approximately SGD 14,500–15,000. Verify the actual rate in IBKR on the day of conversion.

Convert in one transaction on the day the funds arrive. A 3% adverse CHF/SGD move — a large move by historical standards — costs approximately SGD 450 on this amount, which is less than one quarter of one monthly contribution and 0.4% of the SGD 120,000 target. Spreading conversion over months adds complexity and cost that is disproportionate to the risk managed. Convert once, deploy immediately at target weights, and begin the monthly schedule.
IBKR conversion mechanics. Look for a direct CHF-to-USD conversion rate — converting CHF → SGD → USD in two steps costs an additional spread. After conversion, invest at target weights: VWRA 50% · QQQM 25% · SMH 15% · A35 10%. Note: the initial A35 allocation (~10% of SGD 14,750 ≈ SGD 1,475) is sufficient for one board lot (~SGD 1,180). Buy one lot; hold the remaining ~SGD 295 in IBKR cash as the start of the next lot accumulation.

Article IXRebalancing Constitution

Contribution-based rebalancing is the preferred and default approach. Selling to rebalance is constitutionally permitted only in four specific circumstances:

SMH exceeds 20%
Mandatory sell back to 15% target. Execute at the next trading window. No delay, no discretion.
Phase III de-risking
Quarterly sell of QQQM (−3%) and VWRA (−2%), proceeds into A35. Governed by Article XII.
Property acquisition
Liquidation in approved exit sequence to fund the deposit. Governed by Article XIII.
Constitutional amendment
A ratified amendment that changes the approved universe or target allocations may require repositioning.
Outside these four circumstances, you do not sell. Market conditions, underperformance, emotional discomfort, a friend's advice, or a compelling news story do not constitute constitutional authority to sell. If an urge to sell arises outside these conditions, write it in the Decision Journal and wait 72 hours. Most urges do not survive three days.

Property Acquisition Protocol

Capital deployment, phase governance, exit sequence, and CPF interaction.

Article XIIPhase Framework

The portfolio's operating mode shifts automatically as capital approaches the SGD 120,000 target. The trigger is portfolio value versus the target — not the calendar. If markets run well and you reach 85% of target early, you de-risk early. If markets are slow, you stay aggressive longer.

Phase I — Below SGD 72,000 · Full growth
Standard allocation: VWRA 50% / QQQM 25% / SMH 15% / A35 10%. All contributions at target weights per the Decision Engine. Maximum equity exposure — let the portfolio run.
Phase II — SGD 72,000–101,000 · Controlled growth
No selling. Redirect new contributions only: VWRA 55% / QQQM 20% / SMH 10% / A35 15%. Existing holdings unchanged. The portfolio begins tilting toward safety without disrupting the accumulated position.
Phase III — SGD 102,000–113,000 · Progressive preservation
Active de-risking begins. On the first monthly trading window of each quarter, sell enough QQQM and VWRA to reduce their portfolio weights by 3% and 2% respectively, measured against current portfolio value at that window; use proceeds to buy A35. Weight-based, not cashflow-based — the SGD amount varies each quarter as the portfolio grows. Do not reduce SMH — it is reserved for first liquidation at purchase confirmation (Article XIII). The A35 7–13% comfortable range is suspended in this phase; A35 growing above 13% is intentional. Aim toward: VWRA 45% / QQQM 20% / SMH 15% / A35 25%.
Phase IV — Above SGD 114,000 · Property readiness
Stop buying stocks entirely. All new contributions go to A35. Most of the portfolio is now in USD-denominated funds while the goal is SGD — concentrating in A35 reduces the dependence on the exchange rate remaining favourable in the final weeks. Begin planning the purchase timeline. When the date is confirmed and within 60 days, proceed to Article XIII.
Check which phase applies at every monthly trading window. Phases I and II use contribution redirects only — no selling. Phase III uses quarterly selling of QQQM and VWRA. Phase IV stops equity contributions entirely. If the portfolio falls during de-risking and moves back to a lower phase, revert to that phase's rules immediately.
