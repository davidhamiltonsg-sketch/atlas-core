Article I

Purpose and owner context

Silicon Brick Road belongs to Dami. Dami is a Singapore citizen who lives and earns in SGD. It is a medium-term investment portfolio with a flexible horizon and no required end date. It is not constitutionally reserved for a property purchase.

The 9–10% objective is an ambitious planning assumption that requires high equity exposure. It will not be achieved every year and may not be achieved over the eventual holding period.

In plain EnglishThere is no countdown clock. Until a genuine use and date exist, the portfolio remains invested for growth.
Article II

Core beliefs

  • A broad, low-cost passive global core gives the best foundation.
  • Small growth tilts are permitted, but simplicity is a risk control.
  • A market correction is not “bad timing at the end” when no end date is fixed.
  • The most dangerous outcome is creating a deadline after markets fall and then selling under pressure.
  • Return targets guide planning; they never justify leverage or concentration beyond the written caps.

What accumulating means

Each approved UCITS fund is an accumulating share class. When companies or bonds inside the fund pay income, the fund reinvests it automatically. Dami does not receive that income as spendable cash; it is reflected in the fund value. This makes compounding easier, but it does not remove tax-reporting obligations.

Article V

Contributions and rebalancing

  1. Keep emergency and known short-term SGD spending outside SBR.
  2. Route each contribution to the furthest underweight eligible holding.
  3. Use new cash before selling.
  4. Do not wait for a “better” market level when a scheduled contribution is due.
  5. Do not add funds that duplicate an existing job.
  6. Sell only for a hard limit, fund failure, documented exit transition, legal/tax need or constitutional change.

Article V-A

Whole-share purchases and the cash bank

SBR buys whole shares. Any contribution money that cannot purchase another whole share stays in SBR's own cash bank and is added to next month's contribution.

If one whole share plus commission costs S$310, SBR buys three shares for S$930 and carries S$150 forward. The app does not pretend that S$150 was invested.

  • The SBR bank is separate from Atlas.
  • Only confirmed cash and IBKR executions change its balance.
  • Commission and FX are included before deciding how many shares fit.
  • The balance appears as uninvested cash, not as IB01 or an equity holding.

Article VI

Drawdowns

The owner accepts that a portfolio with 95% equity may suffer a severe temporary drawdown and may take years to recover. A fall in price does not reduce the time horizon unless personal circumstances genuinely changed.

During a sharp correction, continue scheduled contributions if personal liquidity is secure. Do not borrow, use margin or increase a satellite above its cap. Wait 72 hours before an unscheduled sale and record the constitutional authority for it.

Article VII

Exit-window protocol

There are no automatic value phases. De-risking begins only when the owner records: the intended use, approximate SGD amount, earliest spending date, latest acceptable date and whether the date can move after a market fall.

  • More than five years away and flexible: growth mode normally continues.
  • Three to five years away: begin a written transition plan for the required amount, not automatically the entire portfolio.
  • One to three years away: progressively match the required spending to SGD-safe assets.
  • Within one year: money that must be spent should not depend on equity or USD/SGD market movements.
The key ruleFirst define the liability. Then match assets to it. Never de-risk merely because a dashboard progress bar reached an arbitrary number.
Article VIII

Currency policy

SGD is the reporting and eventual spending currency. IB01 is acceptable for the entire 5% defensive sleeve in growth mode, but it carries USD/SGD risk and is not equivalent to SGD cash. When a firm SGD liability emerges, the required amount transitions to suitable SGD deposits, bills, money-market or high-quality short-duration holdings.

Do not speculate on FX timing. Currency conversion follows the liability-matching plan.

Article IX

Reviews and amendments

  • Monthly: contribute and record; do not browse for new ideas.
  • Quarterly: review bands, costs and concentration.
  • Annually: confirm objective, emergency liquidity, beneficiaries and whether an exit window now exists.

Material changes require a written case and 30-day cooling-off period. Recent performance, fear of a normal drawdown or a persuasive forecast is not sufficient evidence. Archive every replaced version.

Article X

Continuity

Keep an offline account inventory, fund names and ISINs, beneficiary details and adviser contacts. Store passwords separately. If the owner cannot act, the default is preservation: meet essential cash needs, avoid speculation and restore hard limits only when necessary.
