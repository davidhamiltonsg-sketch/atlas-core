Article I

Purpose and owner context

Atlas Core belongs to David. It exists to compound long-term retirement capital toward 2045. David is an Australian citizen living and earning in Singapore. Tax residence, future residence and estate law can change, so citizenship alone does not determine tax treatment.

In plain EnglishAtlas is long-term capital. Personal SGD liquidity, emergency cash and short-term spending money are held outside it. A temporary fall does not turn long-term capital into short-term capital.

The 10% figure is an ambitious planning objective before personal tax and after product costs. It will not occur smoothly and is not the model’s central expectation. Success means maintaining a sensible process through complete market cycles.

Article II

Investment beliefs

  • Broad, low-cost, rules-based ownership is the foundation.
  • IMID supplies the market-cap equilibrium; IWQU adds a systematic quality tilt rather than manager selection.
  • EQAC, SMH and Bitcoin are explicit views. Their size—not confidence or narrative—is the main defence.
  • ETF labels do not create diversification when they contain the same companies. Look-through exposure governs.
  • Risk includes forced selling, excessive concentration, permanent impairment and abandoning the plan.
  • Monte Carlo, MVO and Black–Litterman concepts are planning tools. Results depend on uncertain returns, volatility and correlations.

Article VI

Contributions, whole shares and the DCA bank

  1. Add each contribution to Atlas’s separate SGD DCA cash bank.
  2. Reserve expected commission and FX costs.
  3. Buy the largest whole-share quantity affordable in the furthest-underweight eligible holding.
  4. Carry every unused dollar into the next contribution cycle.
  5. Use new cash and distributions before selling.
  6. A recommendation does not change the bank; only confirmed cash movements and executions do.

Worked example

If S$3,120 is available after carry-forward and a share costs S$155 after FX with S$5 commission, buy 20 shares for S$3,105 and carry S$15 forward. The remainder is cash, not invested exposure.

Article VII

Crash protocol

A 20%, 30% or 50% fall does not itself permit selling. Continue scheduled contributions while employment, external liquidity and obligations remain secure. Do not borrow, use margin or add leverage. Wait 72 hours before an unscheduled discretionary sale.

The crash sentenceI knew a severe drawdown was possible when I chose a growth portfolio. Pain is not proof that the constitution failed.
Article VIII

Bitcoin sleeve

All direct and listed Bitcoin exposure counts as one 5% sleeve with an 8% hard cap and no mandatory floor. Price, halving narratives and recent performance do not alter the target. New Bitcoin purchases use the approved vehicle after fees, liquidity, custody, tax and estate consequences are checked.

A broken thesis requires durable technical or market-structure failure—not ordinary volatility. Existing underwater wrappers are not sold merely to simplify the dashboard.

Article IX

Currency and defensive assets

David earns and spends mainly in SGD, while Atlas owns global economic exposure and many listings trade in USD. Trading currency is not economic currency. SGD is managed against a basket and is not fixed to USD.

Emergency cash and short-term SGD liabilities remain outside Atlas. When retirement spending becomes foreseeable, the required amount—not an arbitrary percentage—must progressively move to assets matched to the expected spending currency. Long Treasuries may fall with equities during inflation and rising-real-yield shocks; they are not guaranteed crash insurance.

Article X

Quantitative model and its limits

The adopted construct was tested with 100,000 correlated geometric-Brownian-motion paths over 20 years using monthly steps. The base assumptions model approximately 9.14% arithmetic return, 18.29% volatility, a 0.31 Sharpe ratio against a 3.5% hurdle and a 0.70 Sortino ratio. These are model outputs, not forecasts or promises.

The model omits fat tails, volatility clustering, regime changes, liquidity failure, tax changes and permanent correlation shifts. The crucial quality assumption is monitored: if IWQU’s long-run expected return falls below approximately 8.35%, this construct no longer models a higher return than the former allocation. That threshold triggers research at the annual review, not short-term trading.

Article XI

Review calendar and amendments

  • Monthly: contribute, reconcile IBKR, update the DCA bank and act only on the decision ladder.
  • Quarterly: refresh look-through data, inspect costs, bands and concentration.
  • Annually: review objectives, tax residency, beneficiaries, fund documents, model inputs and future currency liabilities.
  • Event-driven: review a fund closure, index change, legal/tax change, genuine technical failure or material personal change.

A strategic change requires a written problem statement, evidence, costs, expected benefit, risks, disconfirming evidence and a 30-day cooling-off period. Recent performance is not sufficient evidence.

Article XII

Continuity and records

Maintain an offline inventory of the broker, accounts, approved fund names and ISINs, beneficiaries, professional contacts and recovery instructions. Keep credentials separately. If David cannot act, preserve liquidity, avoid speculation and make only the minimum trades required to meet genuine obligations or correct a hard breach.

Owner’s acknowledgement

I accept that Atlas can suffer severe temporary losses. I will follow the contribution-first rules, measure underlying concentration and avoid changing strategy in response to fear, excitement or unsupported forecasts.
