Book I

Constitution

Why the system exists, what it believes, and how it governs itself.

Article IPurpose

North Star. The purpose of this Constitution is not to maximise returns. Its purpose is to maximise the probability of reaching retirement with sufficient capital through disciplined, evidence-based investment governance — while eliminating avoidable behavioural mistakes. Markets are uncertain. Governance is permanent.

The Atlas Core Portfolio exists to accumulate retirement capital over a 19-year horizon (2026–2045). All investment activity shall serve this objective and no other. This is not a trading account. It is not a speculative vehicle. It is the machinery of long-term compounding governed by fixed rules that do not bend to headlines, predictions, or emotion.

RiskConcentration is controlled. Hard limits are observed. Uncompensated risk is avoided.
BehaviourEmotional decisions are structurally prevented before they can occur.

Article IIConstitutional Principles

Eight rules for how this Constitution governs itself. When two seem to conflict, the more conservative reading wins.

I
Capital exists to serve a retirement objective. The objective does not exist to serve capital.
II
Investment governance is more durable and more valuable than investment prediction.
III
Diversification is the primary defence against uncertainty — including the uncertainty of conviction.
IV
Risk cannot be eliminated. It can only be accepted, sized, capped, or avoided.
V
Constitutional compliance is more important than short-term performance.
VI
Markets fluctuate continuously. The Constitution shall not fluctuate with them.
VII
Concentration is the enemy. Look-through limits override everything else, including conviction.
VIII
Complexity is permitted only where it demonstrably improves governance. Simplicity wins.

Article IIIGovernance Structure

Although one individual fulfils every function, governance responsibilities are conceptually separated to improve decision quality. When reviewing the portfolio, you are the Investment Committee. When executing a trade, you are the Portfolio Manager. When questioning the rules themselves, you are the Constitutional Reviewer. Keeping these roles distinct prevents the Portfolio Manager from quietly overruling the Committee.

Article IVInvestment Philosophy

The beliefs behind every rule live in Article XXV. Two have operational consequences specific enough to name here:

Shocks are opportunities, not exits

Every major market crash since 1987 has fully recovered. The SGOV buffer exists to deploy capital when everything is falling — not to sit out the fear. When markets drop hard, the instruction is: keep buying, deploy the buffer, sell nothing.

The worst enemy is the portfolio owner

Tinkering, redesign, and emotional selling destroy long-term returns more reliably than any bear market. Article XIV's rules are not supplementary — they are the most important rules in this document. The Constitution's job is to protect you from yourself.

Article VAmendment Process

The Constitution is not amended because markets moved. It is amended because something about your life or financial situation changed in a material and durable way. The amendment process is deliberately slow.

Permitted triggers for a constitutional amendment

Amendment procedure

  1. Identify the trigger. State in writing which permitted trigger applies and why it meets the definition above.
  2. Apply the 72-hour rule. Any constitutional change waits 72 hours from the moment of first proposal before it can be actioned.
  3. Apply the moratorium check. No structural change within 90 days of the last amendment. No more than one discretionary redesign every three years.
  4. Document the change. Record the old rule, the new rule, the trigger that justified it, and the version number increment.
  5. Update the version log in Appendix F. The Constitution takes effect on the date the version log is signed.

Article IXHidden Concentration Limits — the Highest Law

Because VWRA, EQQQ, and SEMI all hold Nvidia, Microsoft, and other large-cap technology companies, your real exposure to those names is larger than any single ETF's percentage suggests. Look-through means adding up what you truly own across all funds combined — not just what each ETF's label shows. This article does that sum and sets hard limits on the result. These limits override everything else, including conviction. A look-through breach is the most urgent action in this portfolio.

Measurement standard (v1.1). A law that cannot be measured is a vibe. Look-through exposure is computed as follows: (1) source — issuer-published full holdings files (Vanguard, Invesco, VanEck, iShares), not third-party aggregators; (2) frequency — recalculated at each dealing window, minimum monthly; (3) method — each constituent's fund weight multiplied by the fund's live portfolio weight, summed across funds; (4) the calculation methodology is version-pinned in the application and any change to it is logged as a governance event. Where issuer files disagree on classification (e.g., what counts as "semiconductor"), the application's pinned taxonomy governs.

Article XBitcoin Cycle Framework

Bitcoin's cap holds at a constant 8% and only ever tightens — it never widens on the cycle. The last halving was 19 April 2024. Phase is tracked for context, but the cap moves in one direction only: down, and only in a sustained deep drawdown.

Post-Halving Bull Phase

12–24 months after halving

8%

Target 7%, soft warning at 8%. The cap does not widen here — same governance as normal. The phase is shown for context only.

Normal Phase

No special halving catalyst

8%

Target 7%, soft warning at 8%. Standard governance. Applies when 24+ months have elapsed and price is within 50% of cycle high.

Bear / Deep Drawdown

Price more than 50% below cycle peak

6%

Target drops to 5%. Cap tightens to protect the portfolio if Bitcoin is in a sustained deep drawdown.

Why the cap never widens on the cycle. Raising the cap during a post-halving window on the belief that "Bitcoin has historically returned most in this phase" would be a market forecast — precisely what the Investment Beliefs Charter (Article XXV) rejects. Increasing exposure because prices are expected to rise is pro-cyclical timing, not governance. The cap is a constant 8% and moves in only one direction: it tightens defensively to 6% in a >50% drawdown (prudence, not prediction). Whether Bitcoin is 6 months or 18 months post-halving changes nothing about the ceiling.
Why 7%? The 7% target is calibrated deliberately. It is large enough to be meaningful — a 3× return on 7% moves the total portfolio by 21 percentage points. It is small enough to survive a complete loss — if Bitcoin went to zero, the portfolio loses 7% and continues. Below 5%, Bitcoin's upside becomes irrelevant to retirement outcomes. Above 8%, Bitcoin drifts toward becoming a structural conviction bet rather than an optionality overlay — a different portfolio with a different risk profile. 7%, capped at 8%, is the range that captures asymmetric upside without making the portfolio dependent on Bitcoin performing.

Article XIIIContribution & DCA Engine

Contribution schedule: USD 3,000 per month, plus a USD 20,000 lump sum each January, growing at 5% per year. Contribution date: 15th of each month. Broker: IBKR Singapore.

Monthly decision sequence — work down this list and stop at the first step that applies

  1. Is any position over its hard cap? → TRIM IMMEDIATELY. This covers: BTC over its cycle cap; EQQQ over 30%; SEMI over 12%; EQQQ+SEMI combined over 42%; any single-stock look-through over its hard limit. Trim to target before anything else. Then proceed to step 8.
  2. Is any position below its target? → 100% of this month's contribution to the most underweight position. Then proceed to step 8.
  3. Is any position above its comfortable range? → Stop adding to it. Redirect the contribution to whichever position is most underweight.
  4. Has a look-through limit been breached? → Schedule a review within 30 days. Keep contributing normally in the meantime.
  5. Is SGOV below 8%? → Build SGOV back to 8% from new contributions — never by selling anything.
  6. Is the portfolio down more than 25% from its all-time high? → Follow the Crash Protocol (Article XIV). Keep contributing. Do not sell.
  7. Everything on track? → Standard monthly investment — split the contribution across positions at target weights: VWRA 52% · EQQQ 23% · SEMI 10% · VFEA 8% · BTC 7%. Skip any position within 3% of its 52-week high (VWRA exempt).
  8. Close the app. Don't monitor prices daily. Come back next month.

High-price exemption — don't buy at the top

Before investing in any position, the system checks whether it is trading within 3% of its 52-week high. If it is, that position is skipped and money is redirected. VWRA is the sole exception — you accumulate it continuously regardless of price. If a position has dropped 12% or more from its recent high, a confirmed buying opportunity exists: deploy using the three-tranche method (30% now → 40% after three green weeks → 30% on confirmed uptrend).

Precedence rule (v1.1). The skip rule applies at step 7 (standard split) only. It does not apply at step 2. When the most underweight position trades within 3% of its 52-week high, buy it anyway — correcting a constitutional drift outranks entry timing. Rationale: the skip rule systematically starves the strongest assets during sustained uptrends and manufactures the very drift the framework then has to repair. Drift correction is law; entry timing is preference. Law wins.

Article XVITrade Execution Standards

  • Manual execution only. The system determines what to do; you place the trade yourself. Where employer policies require pre-approval of trades, obtain that approval first.
  • The dealing window. Normal trades execute between the 3rd business day after the 15th of each month and the last business day of that month. No trades outside this window — except a hard-cap breach, which is actioned on the first available business day after the breach is confirmed, with a governance log entry noting the triggering rule.
  • Minimum hold period. 90 days minimum before selling any position bought. This rule applies to the economic exposure, not the specific instrument. Before buying anything, ask: "Could I hold this through a 20% drop?" If the answer is no, do not buy it.
  • No tranche deployment in under 90 days. After deploying a dip-buying tranche, wait at least 3 months before deploying the next. This prevents averaging into a market that may keep falling.
  • Emergency reserve. Maintain liquid reserves outside this portfolio. Atlas Core is not an emergency fund. No withdrawals before 2045 except genuinely extraordinary, documented circumstances.

Monthly execution checklist

  1. Open the dealing window — confirm it is between the 3rd business day post-15th and the last business day of the month.
  2. Check position caps — has any hard cap been breached? If yes, trim immediately before any other action.
  3. Run the look-through check — are any sector or single-stock limits breached?
  4. Generate the drift-adjusted contribution plan — where does this month's money go?
  5. Execute each trade and log it: date, asset, units, price, total amount, triggering rule.
  6. Update the governance log. Close the app.

Article XXIPre-Committed Shock Responses

Written while calm. When two rules conflict, the more conservative wins — less action, no selling. When shock happens, open this article and follow it.

A Shock Response (pre-decided)
A1Policy shock response.
On a >~10% drop from a discrete event (tariff announcement, geopolitical event, regulatory shock): take no action for 14 calendar days, then deploy the SGOV buffer into the most beaten-down quality holding in three tranches: 30% → 40% → 30%. Sell nothing. Operator-initiated: the application surfaces the A1 checklist (the 14-day countdown and the tranche plan) when a sharp event-driven drop is detected, but does not auto-execute — deployment is a manual dealing-window action. This is distinct from the automatic macro/slow-grind handling (A2).
A2Macro shock response.
On a sustained decline (rate cycle, recession, structural de-rating): hold everything, continue scheduled contributions unchanged, do not redesign. The 2022 rule — the only correct response to a rate-driven bear market is to keep buying.
A3A loss is never a sell trigger.
Being down is a sunk cost. Exit a conviction holding only when its thesis breaks — never on the colour of a number. Ask: "Would I buy this at today's price?" If yes, do not sell.

Broken-thesis criteria — EQQQ: Thesis breaks if US mega-cap tech structurally loses pricing power for 3+ consecutive years AND a credible non-US digital economy alternative emerges at equivalent scale. Valuation de-rating, a rate cycle, or a single bad earnings year are not broken-thesis conditions.

Broken-thesis criteria — SEMI: Thesis breaks if semiconductor capex growth reverses for 3+ consecutive quarters AND AI compute demand enters a sustained structural decline — not a cyclical inventory correction. Cyclical downturns within a secular upcycle are not broken-thesis conditions.

Broken-thesis criteria — BTC: Thesis breaks if (a) a critical protocol-layer vulnerability is confirmed unresolved, or (b) sovereign adoption reverses across 3+ major economies simultaneously with no recovery path, or (c) a technically superior monetary-layer replacement achieves dominant network effect. Regulatory pressure in a single jurisdiction, price drawdown alone, or short-cycle sentiment shifts are not broken-thesis conditions.
A4Never crystallise a loss to fund something else.
Buffers, new positions, and rebalances are funded from new contributions — never by selling an underwater position.
A5Deep-drawdown thesis review.
When a conviction position falls ≥50% below its high, a documented thesis review is triggered — a review, not a sell. Re-test the position against its A3 broken-thesis criteria in writing. If no structural break is confirmed, continue accumulating on weakness toward target. Only if the review is genuinely inconclusive may that one position's accumulation be paused for up to two quarters (contributions redirect to VWRA); the broken-thesis test remains the only exit. This closes the gap between a −50% drawdown and the 3-year broken-thesis threshold without letting price alone force a decision.
B Buying Discipline
B1Don't buy the top.
Do not add to any position within ~3% of its 52-week high. VWRA (the broad-market anchor) is the sole exception. Redirect skipped money to VWRA or hold in SGOV.
B2Three-tranche entry.
Never deploy full intended capital on the first dip signal. Split 30% / 40% / 30%: 30% on the first confirmed dip (≥12% from recent high), 40% after three consecutive green weeks from the trough, 30% once the uptrend confirms. Wait at least 3 months between tranche deployments.
B3Accumulate conviction on weakness.
An underweight conviction position (e.g. BTC below its 7% target) is accumulated on weakness toward target — never exited for being down.
C Structural Limits
C1Hard caps are inviolable.
SEMI ≤ 12%, BTC ≤ its cycle cap, combined EQQQ+SEMI ≤ 42%, and all look-through limits per Article IX. A breach triggers a trim back to target. No debate. No exceptions.
C2Maintain the 8–10% SGOV buffer at all times once built.
Built only from new contributions — never by selling conviction or anchor positions.
C3No half-convictions.
Every position is either sized to matter when it wins or exited. No orphan positions too small to help and large enough to drag performance.
D Anti-Tinkering Throttles
D1One optional trade per quarter (not required by a rule), maximum.
Rule-mandated actions (cap trims, scheduled DCA, emergency rebalances) are unlimited and executed as required. Discretionary changes — new ideas, re-weights, new positions — are capped at one per calendar quarter.
D272-hour cooling-off.
Any action not already mandated by a written rule waits 72 hours before execution. Most urges to act do not survive three days. This is by design.
D3New positions require a written thesis and a one-quarter wait.
No impulse tickers. The six-position structure (five investments + SGOV buffer) is the default. Changing it is deliberately slow and deliberately hard.
E Monthly Cadence
E1The monthly 5-minute check.
Once a month: check hard caps (trim if breached) → check underweights (route the contribution) → check the scheduled-events calendar → act only if a rule fires. Otherwise, close the app. Come back next month.
What to do with the remainder. Monthly contributions rarely divide into exact whole shares — the leftover sits in IBKR cash and rolls forward, combining with next month's allocation for the same position. Do not chase round numbers by topping up; the engine absorbs remainders naturally.
Pre-decide behaviour, automate away the opportunities to override it, and maintain a buffer that requires no prediction. Shocks recover. Accumulated tinkering does not.

Article XXIIICurrency Policy

The portfolio accumulates in USD. The retirement it funds will be lived in SGD. This article governs the relationship between the two over a 19-year horizon.

  • No hedging during accumulation. The holdings are global real assets; their long-run returns embed currency effects, and hedging a 19-year equity horizon costs carry for protection the horizon doesn't need. This is a deliberate acceptance, now written down.
  • Dual-currency measurement. The annual audit (Appendix E) reports portfolio value and the 2045 trajectory in both USD and SGD. The 2045 target is an SGD number — progress is judged in SGD.
  • Natural hedge acknowledgment. Within the Atlas Universe, the SRS pool is SGD-denominated and MPF/ART pools are HKD/AUD — partial natural diversification of the retirement currency exposure. Atlas Core does not duplicate that function.
  • 2040 review mandate. The glide-path review must decide the drawdown-phase currency strategy: what proportion of the distribution portfolio converts to SGD-denominated or SGD-hedged instruments, and on what schedule. Currency risk migrates from "ignorable" to "primary" as the horizon shortens — the 2040 review is where it gets governed.

Article XXIVSuccession & Incapacity

Every governance system needs an answer to one question: who holds this if the operator cannot? This article answers it.

  • Default state on incapacity: freeze. If the operator is unable to act, the portfolio requires no action to remain constitutional. No contributions, no trades, no rebalancing. Every position is designed to be held indefinitely. Doing nothing is the pre-committed instruction.
  • No discretionary authority transfers. No person is authorised to make discretionary trades in the operator's absence. The Constitution does not delegate judgment — it removes the need for it.
  • Continuity documents. A sealed continuity note — broker access instructions, this Constitution's location, the freeze instruction, and estate documentation references — is maintained and its location known to the operator's partner. The note is reviewed at each annual audit.
  • Estate alignment. The Article XV UCITS review (triggered at USD 100k in US-sited assets) is the primary estate-protection mechanism. "Review required" — not "automatically execute" — because laws, IBKR product availability, and tax treatment can change. The review must confirm that migration is still the correct action under current conditions before any trade is placed. Until migration completes, the continuity note must state the US estate-tax exposure explicitly so the estate is not surprised by it.

Article XXVInvestment Beliefs Charter

The Charter defines the permanent assumptions upon which Atlas Core is built. These beliefs are intended to endure across market cycles and may only be amended under Article V when overwhelming empirical evidence demonstrates they are no longer valid. Every article in this Constitution derives from them.

Interpretive supremacy — not operational supremacy. Where the meaning or intent of any rule is ambiguous, it shall be read in the light of this Charter. Where two mechanical rules conflict in operation, the Article XVIII precedence hierarchy resolves the conflict — beliefs guide interpretation and amendment debate, they do not override the ladder. This distinction prevents doctrine from becoming a back door around hard limits.
One voice per subject. Article II covers how the Constitution governs itself. This Charter (XXV) covers what the operator believes about markets. Article IV translates those beliefs into practice. Where the three seem to diverge, this Charter wins on market beliefs; Article II wins on self-governance. No new doctrinal layer may be created.
I
Markets are fundamentally unpredictable. No one can consistently forecast short-term movements well enough to justify abandoning systematic investment. Therefore: no tactical timing, no economic forecasting, no election trading, no recession positioning, no geopolitical speculation. The Constitution assumes uncertainty rather than attempting to eliminate it.
II
Time is the greatest source of advantage. Time compounds both capital and behaviour. The portfolio prioritises remaining invested, continuous accumulation, minimal interruption, and long holding periods. The 2045 horizon is a strategic asset.
III
Behaviour dominates intelligence. Investment failure most commonly results from behavioural mistakes, not analytical ones. Emotions never initiate trades; discretion is constrained; governance exists to protect the investor from himself. This Constitution is primarily behavioural infrastructure.
IV
Diversification is acceptance of humility. It acknowledges that future winners cannot be known. It is not a compromise — it is intellectual honesty. The portfolio deliberately sacrifices maximum upside for greater certainty of reaching 2045.
V
Concentration must always be earned. Conviction increases allocation; it never removes limits. Every concentrated exposure requires a written rationale, explicit sizing, predefined limits, and predefined exit conditions (Articles VII, IX, XXI). No conviction is exempt from governance.
VI
Costs compound permanently. Every dollar not paid in unnecessary fees remains invested forever. The Constitution favours low-cost ETFs, tax efficiency, minimal turnover, and operational simplicity. Saving costs is risk-free return.
VII
Taxes are investment risk. Tax drag permanently reduces compounding. Tax efficiency is considered in every vehicle, domicile, disposal-order, and rebalancing decision (Articles XV, XXVI) — but tax considerations never override constitutional risk management.
VIII
Liquidity creates freedom. Liquidity prevents forced selling. The portfolio maintains reserves — the emergency fund outside Atlas Core, and the SGOV buffer within it — so growth assets are never sold under financial stress. Liquidity exists to protect compounding.
IX
Simplicity is a competitive advantage. Complexity increases mistakes, maintenance, and emotional intervention. New complexity requires positive evidence that governance improves. Complexity without measurable benefit shall be removed.
X
Process outranks outcome. Individual outcomes contain randomness; only repeated execution of sound process can be evaluated. Success is measured by constitutional compliance (Article XXII) before short-term returns.
XI
Capital exists to enable life. The objective of Atlas Core is not wealth accumulation. It is financial independence. Capital is the servant. Life is the master.
XII
Governance is the edge. Products evolve, markets evolve, technology evolves — governance endures. Atlas Core's enduring advantage is not superior prediction. It is superior discipline.

Article XXVITax & Liquidity Constitution

Objective. Taxes and liquidity shall be managed to maximise after-tax wealth while ensuring that no foreseeable financial event requires the involuntary disposal of long-term growth assets.

Tax hierarchy

  1. Avoid taxable events. Contribution-based rebalancing before selling; hold rather than trade.
  2. Prefer tax-efficient vehicles. Domicile, withholding treatment, and estate exposure per Article XV — including the mandated UCITS migration.
  3. Minimise turnover. The dealing-window cadence and 90-day hold are tax rules as much as behavioural ones.
  4. Realise gains only when constitutionally required. Hard-cap trims, mandated vehicle transitions, broken-thesis disposals, material tax-law change, or the liquidity waterfall below. Price appreciation alone never justifies disposal.
  5. Optimise tax after governance — never before. No tax consideration may cause a breach of Articles VII, IX, or XII.

Dividend policy

Cash dividends are never consumed. Unless constitutionally required otherwise, all dividends are reinvested through the monthly contribution engine (Article XIII) as part of that window's deployable amount.

Article XXXConstitutional Crisis Protocol

Articles XX–XXI govern ordinary uncertainty — corrections, bear markets, policy shocks — where the correct response is almost always to hold and keep buying. This article governs the rare systemic event that challenges the framework's own assumptions. Its purpose is not to enable action; it is to impose deliberation and prevent a genuine crisis from becoming an excuse to abandon the Constitution.

The default in a crisis is still the default everywhere: do nothing. A systemic event triggers review, never reflexive de-risking. This article defines how to think when the world appears to be breaking — not a licence to sell.

What counts as a systemic threat

A closed list. Nothing outside it invokes this article; a market crash, a recession, or a scary headline is Article XXI, not Article XXX.

Systemic threatWhat it is (and is not)
Sovereign default of a core marketAn actual default/restructuring of US or a core sovereign — not a debt-ceiling scare or a downgrade.
Supply-chain destruction of a core holdingA Taiwan-type conflict that physically removes a core holding's productive base (e.g. TSMC) — not export controls or a cyclical chip glut.
Base-currency hyperinflationSustained monetary collapse of USD/SGD — not an elevated-CPI cycle.
Permanent deglobalisationA durable structural break in the global equity system VWRA is built on — not a trade war or a bad decade.

The crisis procedure

  1. Name the threat in writing. State which of the four systemic threats applies and the specific, verifiable evidence that it is occurring — not feared, occurring. If it is not on the list, this article does not apply.
  2. Mandatory 14-day deliberation. No structural change for 14 calendar days from the moment the threat is named. Contributions continue on schedule; the buffer may be deployed per Article XXI, but nothing is sold. Panic does not survive two weeks of writing.
  3. Independent consultation. Obtain at least one qualified outside view before any structural action. The Constitutional Reviewer role (Article III) may not both propose and ratify a crisis change.
  4. Test against broken-thesis criteria. A systemic event only justifies exiting a holding if it satisfies that holding's Article XXI(A3) broken-thesis test. A −60% price move does not; a confirmed permanent destruction of the thesis does.
  5. Any change runs the full amendment process. If — after all of the above — a structural change is warranted, it is enacted under Article V and recorded in the Article XXIX decision journal. A crisis is not itself a permitted Article V trigger; only a confirmed thesis break, or a genuine life/regulatory change, is. Absent that, the pre-committed instruction remains: hold, keep contributing, wait.
Why this is deliberately hard to act on. The whole portfolio is built for events that feel existential and turn out not to be. Every systemic scare since 1987 that did not end civilisation also ended the investors who sold into it. This article makes it structurally difficult to convert fear into an irreversible decision — while leaving a real, evidenced, permanent thesis break as the one door that still opens.
